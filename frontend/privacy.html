Privacy Note

Last updated: 2026-07-16

Surfing the Frequencies (LMBoK) is built on a simple rule: no ads, no manipulation, no paywalls, and you own your own data and progress. This note explains what little we do store on your device, and why.

Cookies we use

We use a small number of cookies to keep you signed in and to keep your account secure. We don't use cookies for advertising, tracking across other sites, or analytics profiling — we don't have any of those systems.

CookiePurposeLifetime
fl_accessKeeps you signed in as a learner30 minutes
fl_refreshRenews your session without asking you to log in again30 days
fl_csrfConfirms a request actually came from you, not another website30 days
fl_org_accessKeeps an organization account signed in30 days
fl_org_csrfSame CSRF protection, for organization accounts30 days

These are all "strictly necessary" cookies — they exist only to make login work and to stop other websites from forging requests on your behalf. They aren't used for anything else, and under EU/UK cookie law (and similar rules elsewhere) this category doesn't require a consent banner, so you won't see one here.

Other things stored on your device

  • A small cache of your display name and onboarding status, so the app can greet you correctly before it finishes loading your full profile.
  • Your language and theme preference.
  • Your bioregion (derived from a one-time, city-level location lookup you can decline) — never sent to us or stored on our servers, only kept on your device.

What we store on our servers

Your account details (email, username, display name), your learning progress, and anything you choose to write or contribute (session responses, contributed bioregion portraits, Polis posts, etc.). We don't sell or share this with third parties, and we don't run advertising of any kind.

Questions

This is a small, single-maintainer project. If you have questions about your data, reach out via curious.charb.top.
